# PassionSeed - Legal & Equity Questions

## Overview

This document contains hyper-specific legal and equity questions for the Legal & Equity Session with FI mentors. These questions are tailored to PassionSeed's specific situation: Thai-incorporated, SEA-focused, AI-powered EdTech startup.

---

## Corporate Structure

### Q1: Thai vs. Delaware Incorporation
**Question**: We're currently incorporated in Thailand but planning to raise from international VCs. Should we:
- a) Keep Thai entity and raise via convertible note?
- b) Create a Delaware C-Corp and do a share swap?
- c) Create a Singapore holding company for SEA operations?

**Why it matters**: Affects investor confidence, tax implications, and future exit options.

**Follow-up**: What are the tax implications of each structure for Thai founders?

---

### Q2: SEA Subsidiary Structure
**Question**: We plan to expand to Vietnam, Indonesia, and Philippines. Should we:
- a) Operate as single Thai entity with local partners?
- b) Create subsidiaries in each country?
- c) Use a Singapore holding company with local branches?

**Why it matters**: Affects regulatory compliance, tax efficiency, and operational complexity.

---

### Q3: Data Residency Requirements
**Question**: Each SEA country has different data protection laws (PDPA in Thailand, etc.). Do we need:
- a) Separate data centers in each country?
- b) Can we use a single AWS region (Singapore)?
- c) What are the penalties for non-compliance?

**Why it matters**: Student data is sensitive; non-compliance could block market entry.

## Equity & Co-Founders

### Q4: Co-Founder Equity Split
**Question**: We have 2 technical co-founders. Current split is 75% founder / 25% ESOP. Is this:
- a) Appropriate for 2 co-founders?
- b) Should we adjust to 50/50 with vesting?
- c) How much should we reserve for future hires?

**Why it matters**: Affects founder motivation and investor perception.

---

### Q5: Vesting Schedule
**Question**: What vesting schedule is standard for:
- a) Founders (4-year with 1-year cliff?)
- b) Early employees
- c) Advisors

**Why it matters**: Protects company if co-founder leaves early.

---

### Q6: ESOP Pool Size
**Question**: We have 25% reserved for ESOP. Is this:
- a) Too much? (dilutes founders)
- b) Too little? (can't attract talent)
- c) Should we increase before fundraising?

**Why it matters**: Investors expect 10-20% ESOP pool at seed stage.

## IP & AI Content

### Q7: AI-Generated Content Ownership
**Question**: Our AI generates career quests from expert interviews. Who owns the IP:
- a) PassionSeed (as AI creator)?
- b) The expert (as content source)?
- c) Joint ownership?

**Why it matters**: Could face IP disputes if experts claim ownership.

---

### Q8: Expert Agreement Terms
**Question**: What should our expert agreement include:
- a) IP assignment clause?
- b) Revenue sharing?
- c) Non-compete?
- d) Attribution requirements?

**Why it matters**: Protects our content moat and expert relationships.

---

### Q9: Student Data & Privacy
**Question**: We collect student data (interests, quiz results, career preferences). What do we need:
- a) Parental consent for minors (under 18)?
- b) Privacy policy in Thai + English?
- c) Data retention policy?

**Why it matters**: PDPA Thailand has strict requirements; violations = 5M THB fine.

## Fundraising

### Q10: Convertible Note vs. Equity
**Question**: For our $500K-$1M seed round, should we use:
- a) Convertible note (simpler, faster)?
- b) SAFE (YC standard)?
- c) Priced equity round?

**Question**: What standard rights should we expect investors to request:

- b) Pro-rata rights?
- c) Information rights?
- d) Anti-dilution protection?

**Why it matters**: Could limit founder control if not negotiated properly.

### Q12: Valuation Expectations
**Question**: For a pre-revenue EdTech startup with 1,000+ users, what's a reasonable:
