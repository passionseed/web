## Step 7: Market Analysis

**Task**: For each Customer Problem, list 10 keywords and 10 most interesting resources discovered.

**Submitted Answer**:

### Problem 1: Parents reducing risk of wrong education choices

**Keywords**: education pathway planning, student career assessment, interest-based learning plans, education decision support for parents, student aptitude test platform, personalized education roadmap, college major matching, skills-based education planning, future skills assessment youth, parent decision anxiety education

**Most Interesting Resources**:
- YouScience – Aptitude + interest + labor market alignment; very data-heavy, US school adoption focused.
- Kuder – Old but deeply embedded in US schools; strong assessments, weak experiential layer.
- Crimson Education – Premium positioning; parents buy peace of mind, not discovery.
- MyBlueprint – Visual planning tools for students; light evidence, strong UX.
- OECD Education – Heavy research on skills mismatch and early specialization risk.
- World Economic Forum – Macro justification parents respond to ("jobs don't exist yet").
- Unifrog – UK-focused, strong school distribution, checklist-driven.
- BridgeU – Counselor-facing; data aggregation more than insight.
- Mindler – Emerging market proof that parents will pay directly.
- Harvard Graduate School of Education – Legitimacy source for evidence-backed positioning.

**Takeaway**: Parents pay when you reduce regret risk, not when you promise "finding passion."

### Problem 2: Students safely testing academic & career paths via short projects

**Keywords**: career exploration projects, experiential learning for students, virtual job simulation, project-based career learning, try before you choose education, short-term career programs teens, async learning projects, career discovery platform students, real-world student projects, pre-college career exploration

**Most Interesting Resources**:
- Forage – Gold standard for "safe career testing," but skewed older (college+).
- Outschool – Proof that parents pay for experimentation; lacks structured outcomes.
- Inspirit AI – Narrow domain, strong aspiration pull.
- Polygence – High-touch, expensive, credibility-driven.
- TKS – Community + identity + challenge-based learning.
- XQ Institute – Research + school pilots around real-world learning.
- Coursera – Content-rich, zero identity formation.
- Replit – Accidental career exploration via building.
- MIT Open Learning – Credibility + modular experimentation.
- LinkedIn Learning – Skills-first but not discovery-first.

**Takeaway**: Students don't need more content. They need bounded experiments with reflection loops.

### Problem 3: Counselors needing structured, data-driven student profiles

**Keywords**: school counseling software, student profile dashboard, education data analytics k12, counselor workflow tools, student success platform, caseload management counselors, social emotional learning data, academic advising software, student longitudinal profile, evidence-based counseling tools

**Most Interesting Resources**:
- Naviance – Dominant, bloated, legacy UX; counselors tolerate it.
- Scoir – Modern UX, still destination-focused.
- PowerSchool – Data-rich, insight-poor.
- Clever – Infrastructure layer, potential partner.
- Panorama Education – Strong surveys, weak action linkage.
- ASCA – Distribution gatekeeper.
- Edmentum – Heavy assessment bias.
- Khan Academy – Trusted signal, no counselor tooling.
- IES – Evidence validation source.
- BrightBytes – Proof that schools buy dashboards if tied to outcomes.

**Takeaway**: Counselors want less data, more signal. Anything that saves 10 minutes per student wins.
