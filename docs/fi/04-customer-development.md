## Step 5: Customer Archetype

**Task**: Develop a Customer Archetype for each of the two Customer Problems.

**Submitted Answer**:

### Problem 1: Students safely test real academic and career paths

The ideal counselor customer is a Thai high school student (M4–M6) in a traditional school with limited guidance, who is highly motivated and proactive but lacks a clear roadmap. They are inspired by media, role models, and university environments, and are drawn to hands-on learning such as labs, building, and real projects rather than theory alone. They actively seek camps, open houses, and communities of serious learners, believing that being surrounded by capable people will help them grow. Despite strong family support and high self-belief, they struggle with FOMO, imposter syndrome, and stress caused by unclear benchmarks and repeated comparison with peers.

### Problem 2: Counselors need structured, data-driven student profiles

The ideal counselor customer is a guidance or college counselor aged 30–50 working at an international or urban secondary school in Bangkok, responsible for supporting students' academic planning, personal development, and university preparation. They are experienced, empathetic, and deeply invested in helping students make informed career decisions, often balancing classroom-wide guidance sessions with intensive one-on-one consultations. Their professional life is emotionally demanding, with limited time and energy to provide personalized support, making deep consultations exhausting and difficult to scale.

## Step 6: Existing Solutions

**Task**: Describe how each Customer Archetype currently solves each Customer Problem.

**Submitted Answer**:

### Customer Archetype: Guidance Counselor

**How the customer currently solves the problem**:
Teacher P. addresses student career uncertainty through a combination of general classroom guidance and intensive one-on-one counseling sessions. In class, she provides standardized information about academic pathways, university systems, and application timelines. For deeper issues, she conducts individual consultations to help students navigate personal challenges and long-term career decisions.

**Current steps and tools used**:
- Delivers general guidance lessons using slides and school-provided materials
- Conducts one-on-one counseling sessions (30–60 minutes per student)
- Uses personal experience, questioning, and conversation to uncover student interests
- Relies on informal tools such as notes, worksheets, or ad-hoc reflection exercises
- Refers students to external resources (websites, university pages, open houses)

**Why our solution is multiple times better**:
- Reduces one-on-one session time by 40–60%
- Increases counselor capacity from 1 student/day to 3–5 students/day
- Shifts counseling time from basic discovery to high-value decision-making
- Lowers emotional fatigue by filtering unprepared sessions
- Provides standardized yet personalized insights without additional counselor effort
