# Validation Plan — Sister as Co-Researcher (#1)

**Date:** 2026-05-28
**Context:** Companion to [`2026-05-28-problem-context.md`](2026-05-28-problem-context.md). Highest-ROI validation path given current access: one M4 sister (just chose her track) + one M3 contact (deciding now) + referral chains through them.

---

## Goal

Turn sister into recruiter + facilitator. She runs the card game on her network (M4 friends + her M3 contact + their friends) → **10–15 students in 2 weeks, zero cold outreach.** Output: behavioral data on the problem + warm recruits for validation paths #2 and #3.

**Why sister is the lever:** trust transfers through her, not through you. A 30-something founder asking M3 kids about their future = suspicious. The sister's friend = safe. She also multiplies access — your single biggest bottleneck.

---

## Sister's Brief

Her role, plain:

- **Recruiter** — invite friends, vouch for the project (trust flows through her)
- **Facilitator** — run the card game, ask the questions, write answers down
- **NOT a salesperson** — she is not pitching PassionSeed. She's "doing a fun thing + helping her brother's project."

### 3 rules to drill in

1. **Don't lead.** Never say "it's hard to choose your track, right?" — that plants the answer. Ask open, let them talk.
2. **Parent out of the room.** Kid + sister only. A parent present = the kid gives safe, parent-approved answers and the bias data is destroyed.
3. **Write what they SAY, not what she thinks.** Verbatim. "Idk lol" is data. Don't clean it up.

---

## Recruitment Script (sister → friends)

Casual, in her own voice (Thai in real life):

> "doing a quick career card game thing for my brother's project — like a personality test but about jobs / your future track. 20 min, kinda fun, you get a little reading at the end. wanna try? can do it over call or in person."

Snowball ask after **every** session:

> "this was helpful! know 1–2 friends also figuring out their track? send them my way 🙏"

The snowball ask is the multiplier. Never skip it.

---

## Session Flow (~25 min, sister runs)

**0. Warmup (2 min)** — "No right answers, just be honest, this isn't graded." Lower the stakes → truer answers.

**1. Card rank (3 min)** — show the 4 cards, rank 1–4. Write the order + ask "why #1?"

**2. Distract (10 min)** — run the other questions here (steps 4–5). The gap itself is the distraction.

**3. Re-rank (1 min)** — "rank them again, fresh." Compare to step 1. **A flip is data.**

**4. Label-vs-labor (5 min)** — "what job or track are you thinking about?" → "describe one work day, morning to evening, hour by hour." Write it down. (Later, compare to a real day-in-the-life.)

**5. Decision + influence (4 min)**
- "Have you picked your track? How sure, 1–10? Why that number?"
- "Who did you talk to about it?" (let them list — do NOT prompt "a counselor?")
- "Anyone you didn't talk to but wish you could?"

## Kill Criteria — Watch For

**Problem confirmed if:**
- Card flip rate is high (can't rank values stably)
- Imagined work day ≠ real labor (label-vs-labor gap)
- "Who they talked to" = parents / friends / TikTok, never a counselor
- Confidence low, OR high but with bad reasoning

**Thesis weaker if the opposite holds** — stable ranks, accurate labor, real structured guidance, confident with good reasoning. If so, say it honestly and rethink the framing. Validation that can't fail isn't validation.
