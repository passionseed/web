## Overview

**Event Name**: The Next Decade Hackathon (TNDH) 2026  
**Theme**: Preventive & Predictive Healthcare  
**Tagline**: "Anyone Can Make an Impact"  
**Focus**: Healthcare innovation focusing on preventive, predictive, evidence-based, and empathetic solutions

### Core Philosophy

**Preventive Healthcare** (การป้องกัน): Reduce chances of problems before they occur  
**Predictive Healthcare** (การคาดการณ์): Use data/behavior to predict risks in advance

**Framework**: Predict → Detect Early → Intervene → Prevent

Unlike traditional reactive healthcare systems, TNDH focuses on:
- Working **before problems occur** (ก่อนเกิดปัญหา)
- Population-level scaling
- Integration into daily life

## Challenge Tracks & Problems

### Track 01: Traditional & Integrative Healthcare
**Icon**: HeartPulse  
**Color**: #91C4E3 (Cyan/Blue)

#### Problem P1: The Last-Mile Chronic Disease Gap
- **Challenge**: How might we design a low-cost, community-deployable screening tool that enables rural communities to detect chronic disease risk — without requiring hospital infrastructure?
- **Context**: Rural communities face chronic diseases but lack early detection tools
- **Impact**: Real-world equivalent to building a "health radar" for villages

#### Problem P2: The Traditional Medicine Data Desert
- **Challenge**: How might we create a bridge that digitizes traditional medicine outcomes and makes them interoperable with modern health records — enabling integrated, evidence-based care?
- **Context**: Traditional medicine lacks systematic data collection and integration
- **Impact**: Creating a "translator" between traditional and modern healthcare systems

### Track 02: Mental Health
**Icon**: Brain  
**Color**: #A594BA (Purple)

#### Problem P4: The Stigma Wall (กำแพงแห่งอคติ)
- **Context**: 37% of Thai students experience burnout, but more than 85% have never sought help out of fear of social judgment
- **Challenge**: How might we design a destigmatized early mental health detection and support system that meets young people where they are — without labeling or exposing them?
- **Tangible Equivalent**: "Like having a broken leg, but society tells you it's 'just in your head'"
- **Scores**: Severity 9, Difficulty 8, Impact 9, Urgency 9
- **Tags**: Destigmatization, Youth, Early Detection

#### Problem P5: Connected But Alone (เชื่อมต่อแต่โดดเดี่ยว)
- **Context**: Teen loneliness in Thailand has reached crisis levels despite increased social media use — because online connections don't resolve real-life social isolation
- **Challenge**: How might we design an intervention that addresses root-cause social isolation — not just surface-level connection — for teenagers and young adults?
- **Tangible Equivalent**: "The mortality impact is equivalent to smoking 15 cigarettes a day"
- **Scores**: Severity 9, Difficulty 8, Impact 10, Urgency 10
- **Tags**: Social Isolation, Teenagers, Intervention

#### Problem P6: Mental Healthcare in the Last Mile (สุขภาพจิตในพื้นที่ห่างไกล)
- **Context**: Rural Thailand has only 1 psychiatrist per 200,000 people, leaving those who need help with no way to access care
- **Challenge**: How might we build a scalable, culturally appropriate mental wellness support system for underserved communities — where professional help is inaccessible?
- **Tangible Equivalent**: "Imagine 1 doctor trying to treat a packed stadium of 200,000 people"
- **Scores**: Severity 9, Difficulty 8, Impact 9, Urgency 9
- **Tags**: Access, Rural Communities, Scalable Care

---

### Track 03: Community, Public & Environmental Health
**Icon**: Globe  
**Color**: #91C4E3 (Light Blue)

#### Problem P7: Data Rich, Action Poor (ข้อมูลมากมาย ไหนล่ะการกระทำ?)
- **Context**: Real-time air quality data is already available, but communities can't translate that data into protective actions
- **Challenge**: How might we turn real-time environmental health data into actionable community behavior change — at the neighborhood level, not just on a dashboard?
- **Tangible Equivalent**: "Like having a fire alarm that rings, but gives you no water to put out the fire"
- **Scores**: Severity 9, Difficulty 7, Impact 9, Urgency 8
- **Tags**: Environmental Data, Behavior Change, Community

#### Problem P8: The Food Safety Blind Spot (อร่อยให้หก สกปรก...หรือเปล่า?)
- **Context**: 40% of Thai street food markets lack consistent food safety inspection, resulting in tens of thousands of food-related illnesses per year
- **Challenge**: How might we design a community-powered food safety monitoring and early warning system that works without requiring top-down government enforcement?
- **Tangible Equivalent**: "Like playing Russian Roulette with your lunch every day"
- **Scores**: Severity 8, Difficulty 7, Impact 8, Urgency 7
- **Tags**: Food Safety, Community Monitoring, Public Health

#### Problem P9: PM2.5 vs. Our Children (เมืองหลวงควันและฝุ่นมากมาย)
- **Context**: PM2.5 dust disproportionately affects school children, but most schools lack timely warning systems or prevention plans
- **Challenge**: How might we build a predictive PM2.5 alert and response system that triggers preemptive protective actions for schools and children — before dangerous exposure occurs?
- **Tangible Equivalent**: "Like forcing 13.6 million kids to smoke cigarettes before recess"
- **Scores**: Severity 9, Difficulty 7, Impact 9, Urgency 10
- **Tags**: Air Quality, Children, Predictive Alerts

## Judging Criteria

### From Codebase (Challenge Page)
| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Impact** | 30% | Does it address a real health problem? What's the scale of potential impact? |
| **Innovation** | 25% | Is the approach novel? Does it combine ideas in an unexpected way? |
| **Feasibility** | 25% | Can this be built? Is the prototype functional or clearly demonstrable? |
| **Prototype Quality** | 20% | How well does the demo communicate the solution? UX, clarity, completeness |

### From Official Guidebook (More Detailed)
| Criteria | Weight | Description (Thai) |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| **Impact** | 25% | Impact of the problem found through research and user interviews |
| **Solution Effectiveness** | - | Ability of solution to actually solve the stated problem, aligned with user pain points |
| **Concept Relevance** | 20% | Alignment with track and preventive & predictive concepts, appropriateness of chosen approach |
| **Product Readiness** | - | Prototype readiness - can be actually tested, clear demonstration, not just concept/slides |
| **Presentation** | 5% | Clarity and ease of understanding in video communication, storytelling, and content organization |

## Important Notes for AI Assistants

1. **Challenge Details**: Full problem statements with severity, difficulty, impact, and urgency scores are stored in the database (`hackathon_challenges` table) and displayed dynamically

2. **Language Support**: All content has both English (en) and Thai (th) versions

3. **Problem Tags**: Each challenge has associated tags for categorization (e.g., "rural health", "data integration", "mental wellness")

4. **Real-World Impact**: Each problem includes a "tangible equivalent" - a real-world analogy to help participants understand the scope

5. **Questionnaire Data**: Participants select 1-3 problem preferences during onboarding, which informs team matching

6. **Team Matching**: System considers problem preferences and role preferences when suggesting team formations

7. **Design Thinking Process**: The hackathon follows a structured innovation methodology, not just free-form hacking

8. **Two Judging Criteria Sets**: The codebase shows one set (Impact 30%, Innovation 25%, Feasibility 25%, Prototype 20%), while the official guidebook shows another (Impact 25%, Solution Effectiveness, Concept Relevance 20%, Product Readiness, Presentation 5%). Both may be used in different evaluation stages.
