## Title
What You'll Walk Away With

## Grading Mode
orientation

## Learning Goal
Students understand what Phase 1 is asking them to do and why. They should be able to name the problem space they want to explore and articulate why it feels hard or uncertain. Honest confusion is a valid and expected output at this stage.

## What To Look For
- Names a specific problem space or topic they want to explore
- Shows genuine engagement with the framing (not just restating the instructions)
- Expresses honest uncertainty, curiosity, or a real question they are sitting with
- Demonstrates they understand this phase is about understanding a system, not finding a solution fast

## Red Flags
- Completely blank or copy-pasted from the instructions
- Already pitching a solution with no problem exploration
- Generic filler with no personal connection to the topic

## Grading Guidance
This is an orientation activity. Do NOT apply the Reality Signal Check, Artifact Type Check, or Phase Alignment Check. There is no interview evidence expected here. The student is not yet supposed to have talked to anyone.

Grade on genuine engagement only: did they name a real problem space and show honest thinking about it? A student who says "I feel stuck because I cannot see a solution" is showing exactly the right mindset for this phase. That is a pass.

The bar is low but real: blank submissions or pure solution-pitching fail. Everything else that shows genuine reflection passes.
