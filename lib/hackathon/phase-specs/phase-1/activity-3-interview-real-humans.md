## Title
Interview Real Humans

## Learning Goal
Gather behavioral evidence from real people through interviews or observation. Students should show they talked to actual humans and extracted concrete stories about what happened, not just opinions or abstract complaints.

## What To Look For
- At least 5 distinct interviews or observations documented
- Questions focus on specific past behavior ("what happened last time?") not hypotheticals
- Captures concrete stories with details: who, what, when, where
- Notes surprising or disconfirming findings (not just confirmation)
- Distinguishes observed behavior from stated opinion
- Evidence is tied to a specific target user group

## Red Flags
- Hypothetical questions ("what would you want?", "do you think this is a good idea?")
- Fewer than 5 interviews with no depth
- All interviews confirm the student's pre-existing idea
- No specific stories — only generic summaries ("people said they were frustrated")
- Interviewing friends/family without justification
- No documentation of what was actually asked or observed

## Exemplars
Strong: "Interviewed 7 food delivery riders. Asked: 'Walk me through your last delivery.' Pattern: 4/7 described parking fines as bigger cost than fuel. Surprising: 2 riders said they prefer rainy days because fewer police checks. One described hiding in a specific soi to avoid a recurring checkpoint."

Weak: "I asked 3 friends if they would use my app. They all said yes. People want convenience."

## Grading Guidance
Quality of evidence matters more than quantity. 3 deep interviews with concrete stories beat 10 shallow ones. Look for surprise — if every interview confirms exactly what the student expected, they probably weren't probing deeply enough. Be skeptical of friend/family interviews unless the target user is genuinely that narrow.
